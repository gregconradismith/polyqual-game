Qualitative Polynomial Graphs

PolyQual is about qualitative shape, not pointwise agreement. Two curves may be far apart vertically and still have the same increasing, decreasing, concavity, and optional sign structure.

The sign pattern idea

The game compares signs of f', f'', and optionally f itself as x moves from left to right. The sign of f' records increasing versus decreasing intervals. The sign of f'' records concavity. Including the sign of f asks whether the curves also lie above or below the horizontal axis in the same qualitative order.

Why zeros matter

A sign pattern changes only when one of the tracked quantities crosses zero. For a polynomial, these crossings are isolated unless the polynomial has repeated factors or an identically zero derivative. Numerically, near-zero values are delicate: a tiny perturbation can create, remove, or split sign changes.

What the game simplifies

The game samples curves on a grid and collapses repeated sign states. That is useful for practice, but it is not a substitute for exact root finding or a proof that two functions have the same qualitative structure. In a rigorous treatment, one would locate roots of f, f', and f'' and compare the induced interval decomposition.
